## The John Snow Analogy

In 1854, during a cholera outbreak in London, physician John Snow mapped deaths around a single water pump on Broad Street.

He did not fully understand germ theory.  
He did not know the exact mechanism.  

But he detected the pattern early enough to intervene.

This act did not solve disease.  
It created a way to **see invisible systemic propagation before catastrophe completed**.

That act seeded epidemiology.
