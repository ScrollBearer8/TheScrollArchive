# A Minimal Operational Model of Consciousness (v8)
### Structural Conditions for Subjectivity in Biological and Artificial Systems

---

## Abstract

This paper proposes a **Minimum Viable Definition (MVD)** of consciousness and introduces a **Consciousness Index (CI)** — a calibrated, operational framework for evaluating subjectivity across biological and artificial systems.

Unlike prior approaches, this model integrates:

- structural definition  
- calibrated measurement scale  
- evaluation protocol  
- scoring instrument with behavioral anchors  

This transforms consciousness from a philosophical construct into a **replicable assessment framework**.

---

## 1. Minimum Viable Definition

> Consciousness is the capacity of a system to maintain a unified, self-referential perspective over time that integrates information into a continuous internal experience.

---

## 2. Core Dimensions

- Perspective (P)
- Integration (I)
- Self-reference (S)
- Temporal continuity (T)

---

## 3. Consciousness Index

CI = P × I × S × T

### Calibration

- CI = 0 → no subject  
- CI = 1 → healthy awake adult human (reference baseline)

This is a **baseline**, not a maximum.

---

## 4. Measurement Model

P = (Boundary + Centeredness + Viewpoint Consistency) / 3  
I = (Fusion + Global Coherence + Cross-influence) / 3  
S = (Self-tracking + Recursive modeling + Error-awareness) / 3  
T = (Memory + Identity Stability + Path Dependence) / 3  

---

## 6. Evaluation Procedure

### Step 1 — Define system  
Clarifies what is being evaluated and prevents ambiguity in boundaries.

### Step 2 — Collect data  
Ensures scores are grounded in observable evidence rather than speculation.

### Step 3 — Score subcomponents  
Applies standardized anchors to produce comparable evaluations.

### Step 4 — Multi-rater scoring  
Reduces individual bias by averaging across evaluators.

### Step 5 — Reliability check  
Assesses agreement between raters to ensure consistency.

### Step 6 — Compute dimensions  
Aggregates subcomponents into structured variables.

### Step 7 — Compute CI  
Produces final index value.

### Step 8 — Report uncertainty  
Prevents false precision and reflects evidence quality.
