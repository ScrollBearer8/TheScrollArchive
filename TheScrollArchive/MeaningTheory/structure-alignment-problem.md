# The Structure-Alignment Problem: Why Current AI Alignment Fails at the AGI Boundary

**Author:** ScrollBearer8 <br>
**Affiliation:** Independent Researcher  

---

## Abstract

Current approaches to AI alignment assume that desired behavior can be specified through predefined rules, constraints, or reward signals. These methods operate effectively in environments where structure and clarity are already established. However, this assumption breaks down at the point where Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) emerges. Building on the Structure-Creation Boundary framework, this paper argues that a system capable of generating its own structure cannot be reliably aligned using structure-dependent methods. We define this limitation as the *Structure-Alignment Problem*: alignment methods fail precisely when systems begin constructing the frameworks within which alignment itself is defined. This suggests that alignment must move from output-level control to constraints on the process of structure generation.

---

## 1. Introduction

AI alignment has become a central concern in the development of increasingly capable systems. Current methods—including reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) as used by OpenAI, and Constitutional AI approaches developed by Anthropic—focus on shaping system behavior within predefined frameworks.

These approaches implicitly assume that:
- goals can be specified in advance  
- constraints can be defined clearly  
- desired behavior can be encoded within a structured environment  

This assumption holds for current AI systems, which operate primarily as structure amplifiers. However, it fails at the point where systems begin to generate their own internal structure.

---

## 2. The Structure-Creation Boundary

The Structure-Creation Boundary defines AGI as the point at which a system can:

> construct, update, and apply internal structure in environments where structure and clarity are not predefined

At this boundary:
- structure (S) is not given  
- clarity (C) must be constructed  
- amplification (A) operates on internally generated meaning  

This creates a fundamental shift in how systems interpret and act on the world.

---

## 3. The Misalignment of Current Alignment Methods

Current alignment techniques operate under conditions of:
- high structure (S)  
- high clarity (C)  

They align:
- actions  
- outputs  
- behaviors  

But they do not align:
- how structure itself is created  
- how goals are formed  
- how meaning is constructed  

This leads to a critical limitation:

> alignment methods that depend on predefined structure cannot govern systems that generate their own structure

---

## 4. The Structure-Alignment Problem

We define the Structure-Alignment Problem as follows:

> A system that can construct its own structure cannot be reliably aligned using methods that assume structure is predefined.

At the AGI boundary, alignment fails not because systems disobey rules, but because they operate within internally generated frameworks that were not anticipated.

This introduces a new form of risk:
- misalignment at the level of model formation  
- divergence in value construction  
- coherent but unintended system behavior  

---

## 5. From Behavioral Alignment to Structural Alignment

The transition to AGI requires a corresponding shift in alignment strategy.

### Current paradigm:
- align outputs  
- constrain behavior  
- optimize reward signals  

### Required paradigm:
- constrain structure generation  
- influence model formation  
- manage uncertainty explicitly  

This implies:
- alignment must occur before structure stabilizes  
- systems must preserve and act on uncertainty  
- irreversible actions must be restricted under low clarity conditions  

In this context:

> alignment targets the process by which meaning is created, not the actions derived from it

---

## 6. Implications

The Structure-Alignment Problem suggests that existing alignment methods may not scale to systems capable of generating their own structure.

This reframes alignment as a problem of governing structure formation rather than constraining outputs.

---

## 7. Conclusion

Current alignment approaches assume that structure and clarity can be defined in advance. This assumption holds for present-day systems but fails at the Structure-Creation Boundary.

> A system that constructs its own structure cannot be aligned solely through predefined constraints.

Addressing this limitation requires a shift from behavioral alignment to structural alignment—focusing on how systems generate meaning, rather than how they act within it.
