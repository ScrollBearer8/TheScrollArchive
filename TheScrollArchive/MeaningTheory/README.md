## Meaning Theory (Descriptive Layer)

Meaning Theory defines:

> **M = S × A × C**

Where:

- **S (Structure)** = coherence of the underlying model  
- **A (Amplification)** = scale and propagation capacity  
- **C (Clarity)** = precision and bounded interpretation  

In the non-agentive limit:

> **M = S × C**

---

### Interpretation

Meaning Theory is:

- descriptive  
- neutral  
- non-normative  

It answers:

> **What remains stable under amplification?**

It does **not** determine whether a structure is desirable or safe.

---

## Structural Alignment (Applied Layer)

Structural Alignment extends Meaning Theory to address safety and compatibility.

We define:

> **M\* = S × A × C − λP**

Where:

- **P** = degeneracy penalty (coercion, manipulation, monoculture, etc.)  
- **λ** = penalty weight  

---

### Interpretation

Structural Alignment answers:

> **Which stable structures should be allowed to scale?**

It introduces:

- evaluation  
- filtering  
- intervention  

---

## Shared Meaning (SC)

Alignment is defined as the preservation of:

> **Shared Meaning = sustained compatibility of structure and interpretation between humans and AI**

Where:

- **S** ensures structural compatibility  
- **C** ensures interpretive compatibility  

---

## Core Concepts

- **SC (Structure + Clarity):** substrate of shared meaning  
- **M:** descriptive meaning (what holds)  
- **M\*:** aligned meaning (what should scale)  
- **SC Attractor:** stable structure systems converge toward  
- **SC Drift:** loss of shared meaning over time  

---

## Summary

This framework proposes:

- AGI alignment is a **trajectory problem**, not a control problem  
- Systems converge toward **stable internal structures (SC attractors)**  
- Alignment requires guiding convergence toward **human-compatible shared meaning**  
- Measurement (M\*) and monitoring (SC Drift) are required to prevent divergence
